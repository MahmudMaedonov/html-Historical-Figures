Great People That Changed The World!

  • Albert Einstein (1879–1955)

    • Einstein was a German-born physicist who developed the theory of relativity, changing how people understand time and space. His famous equation E = mc² explains how energy and mass are connected. He also won the Nobel Prize in Physics in 1921 for discovering the photoelectric effect.

  • Marie Curie (1867–1934)

    • Marie Curie was a Polish-French scientist who discovered radium and polonium, two radioactive elements. She was the first woman to win a Nobel Prize, and the only person to win Nobel Prizes in two different sciences (Physics and Chemistry). Her research helped develop X-ray machines.

  • Isaac Newton (1643–1727)

    • Newton was an English mathematician and physicist. He discovered the laws of motion and gravity, explaining how objects move on Earth and in space. He also invented calculus and built the first practical reflecting telescope.

  • Nelson Mandela (1918–2013)

    • Mandela was a South African leader who fought to end apartheid, a system that separated people by race. After spending 27 years in prison, he became South Africa’s first Black president in 1994 and worked for peace and equality.

  • Abraham Lincoln (1809–1865)

    • Lincoln was the 16th President of the United States. He led the country during the Civil War and worked to end slavery with the Emancipation Proclamation. He believed in freedom and equality for all people.

  • Queen Elizabeth I (1533–1603)

    • Elizabeth I ruled England for 45 years during the Elizabethan Era, a time of great art and exploration. She supported famous explorers like Sir Francis Drake and writers like William Shakespeare, helping England grow stronger and more creative.

  • Martin Luther King Jr. (1929–1968)

    • Dr. King was an American minister who led the Civil Rights Movement in the 1960s. He believed in nonviolent protest and gave the famous “I Have a Dream” speech. His work helped end racial segregation in the U.S.

  • Mahatma Gandhi (1869–1948)

    • Gandhi was a leader from India who fought for independence from British rule using peaceful protests. His ideas of nonviolence and truth inspired movements for civil rights all over the world.

  • Malala Yousafzai (1997–Present)

    • Malala is a Pakistani activist who stood up for girls’ education when she was just a teenager. After surviving an attack by extremists, she continued her fight for education and became the youngest person to win the Nobel Peace Prize.

  • Leonardo da Vinci (1452–1519)

    • Da Vinci was an Italian artist, inventor, and scientist. He painted the Mona Lisa and The Last Supper, two of the most famous paintings in history. He also designed inventions like flying machines and studied anatomy and nature.

  • William Shakespeare (1564–1616)

    • Shakespeare was an English playwright and poet known as one of the greatest writers of all time. He wrote plays like Romeo and Juliet, Hamlet, and Macbeth, which are still performed around the world today.

  • Frida Kahlo (1907–1954)

    • Frida Kahlo was a Mexican painter known for her colorful self-portraits that expressed her pain, identity, and strength. She became a symbol of feminism and Mexican culture, showing how art can tell powerful personal stories.
